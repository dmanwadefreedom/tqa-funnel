## DESIGN NOTES FOR SLIDE BUILDER

**Tone:** Corporate briefing. Think McKinsey, not marketing webinar.

**Color palette:** Dark navy or charcoal background. White and light gray text. One accent color (steel blue or teal) used sparingly for emphasis.

**Typography:** Clean sans-serif. Headings in medium weight, not bold. No ALL CAPS except the title slide.

**Imagery:** Minimal. No stock photos of people at computers. No padlock icons. If any images are used, they should be data visualizations or clean diagrams.

**Animations:** None. Slide transitions should be simple cuts or subtle fades.

**What to avoid:**
- Exclamation marks anywhere in the deck
- Words: "amazing," "incredible," "game-changing," "transform," "unleash"
- Emoji of any kind
- Testimonial slides (there are none — DJ hasn't collected them yet)
- Pricing, bonuses, stacking, or urgency language
- "Limited spots" or "only X remaining"
- Any visual that looks like an infomercial

**What this deck should feel like:**
A senior executive walked into a room, shared exactly what he knows, answered questions honestly, and mentioned at the end that he runs a program if anyone wants help. That's it. The authority of the content IS the selling mechanism.
