## 2. LONG-FORM CONTENT FRAMEWORK: YOUTUBE + PODCAST

### The Victoria Model Applied to DJ

Victoria's method: Make YouTube videos so specific that the RIGHT person watches and self-qualifies. No bait. No "free masterclass" hook. Just brutally useful content that demonstrates you know what you're talking about.

What Victoria did to Dylan: He watched her break down EXACTLY what she does. He thought "this person clearly knows her stuff and I want what she's offering." He came to her ready to pay. No funnel tricks needed.

**DJ's version of this:** Make videos where IT professionals watch and think "this guy has been in the room where I want to be. He knows exactly what I need to do. I should work with him."

### The 2-Minute Question

DJ wants 2-minute videos. Here is the honest answer:

**2 minutes is fine for SHORT-FORM (LinkedIn, Instagram Reels, TikTok, Shorts).** That is distribution content -- it gets eyeballs.

**2 minutes is NOT enough for YouTube trust-building.** Victoria's videos that converted Dylan were not 2 minutes. Boyd Clewis's videos that convert at 318K subscribers are not 2 minutes. The data is clear:

- YouTube videos 10-20 minutes long get 2x the watch time of videos under 5 minutes
- The algorithm promotes longer videos because they keep people on the platform
- Trust is built through DEPTH, not speed. 2 minutes proves you can talk. 15 minutes proves you know what you are talking about.
- Boyd's top-performing videos: "How I got into cybersecurity" (23 min), "GRC career roadmap" (18 min), "CISSP study plan" (31 min)

**THE SOLUTION: Both.**

| Format | Length | Platform | Purpose |
|--------|--------|----------|---------|
| Deep-dive | 12-18 min | YouTube (main upload) | Trust-building. This is where people decide to buy. |
| Key clip | 2-3 min | YouTube Shorts + LinkedIn + Reels | Distribution. Drives traffic to the full video. |
| Audio version | Full length | Spotify (Cyber Caffeine) | Same content, audio only. Repurpose, not recreate. |

**DJ records ONE 15-minute video. From that, the team extracts:**
1. The full video (YouTube)
2. 2-3 short clips (Shorts, LinkedIn, Reels)
3. Audio track (Spotify podcast episode)
4. Text summary (LinkedIn article / newsletter)

One recording session = 4-6 pieces of content across all platforms.
