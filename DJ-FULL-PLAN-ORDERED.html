Why this doesn't feel like a pitch

  • The document does NOT mention Cybersecurity Accelerator. It doesn't sell anything.

  • At the very bottom, one line: "If you want help executing this plan, you can apply here."
  • The person who can do it alone does it alone. Got their money's worth.
  • The person who reads the plan and thinks "I need someone to walk me through this" applies.
  • Both people are happy. Nobody feels used.

Application Form (who's serious)

FREE

The assessment PDF ends with an apply link. They've already paid $47 and read their personalized plan. They know exactly what the gap is. Now they decide if they want help closing it.

The application asks 5-8 questions

  • Current role and years of experience
  • Target role and salary goal
  • When do you want to make the switch? (This month / This quarter / This year / Someday)
  • Are you ready to invest in your career transition? (Yes / Not yet)
  • How did you find DJ?

GHL auto-scores every application (0-12 points). Hot leads (9-12) get flagged to DJ within 24 hours. Warm leads (5-8) get an email sequence. Cold leads (0-4) get the newsletter.

15-Minute Strategy Call (DJ decides)

FREE for qualified applicants

Only people who scored 9+ on the application get a call with DJ. This is not a sales pitch. DJ reviews their application, asks a few questions, and gives honest advice.

How the call goes

  • "I saw your application. Tell me about where you are right now."
  • "Based on what you've told me, here's what I'd recommend."
  • If they're a fit: "I run a 14-week program called Cybersecurity Accelerator. I think you'd be a good fit. Here's how it works."
  • If they're not ready: "Here's what I'd do first. When you're ready, come back."
  • 15 minutes. DJ has already seen their quiz score, their assessment results, and their application. He knows who he's talking to before the call starts.
