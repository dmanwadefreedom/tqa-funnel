Step 2: DJ's 15-Minute Call

This is NOT a sales call. It's a fit check. DJ has already seen their quiz score, assessment results, and application. He knows who he's talking to before the call starts.

Call script (DJ's words)

"Hey [name], thanks for taking the time. I read your application — I can see you've been in [their role] for [X years]. Tell me a bit more about where you're at right now."

Listen. Let them talk. 3-4 minutes.

"Based on what you've told me, here's what I'd recommend. [Give specific advice based on their background — which path, which certs, realistic timeline.]"

Give real value. 3-4 minutes. They should feel like this call alone was worth their time.

If they're a fit:
"I run a 14-week program called CISO 360. Based on what you've told me, I think you'd be a good fit. It's $4,997 — or you can split it into three payments. We work with you until you're hired. Not 14 weeks. Until. Would you like me to send you the details?"

If yes → send Stripe link. If they need time → "Take a few days. I'll follow up Friday."

If they're not ready:
"Honestly, I don't think you're ready for this right now. Here's what I'd do first: [specific advice]. When you've done that, come back and we'll talk again."

Turning people away builds more trust than enrolling everyone. Word gets around.
