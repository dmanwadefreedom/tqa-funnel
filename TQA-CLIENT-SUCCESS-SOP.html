The Model

Victoria Shearing's Organic Funnel (adapted for DJ)

Victoria has 315 YouTube subscribers and 10 videos. She gets 2,200 views on a single video and books sales calls from it. Her model: post content on IG to catch attention, send them to YouTube to build trust, every video links to Calendly, close on the call.

We're taking that framework and fitting it to DJ's situation. DJ has 28 years of real CISO experience, two student success stories, world-class case studies, and 9K LinkedIn followers already. The content writes itself. He just needs to press record.

IG (catch attention) > YouTube (build trust) > Application > 15-min Call > CISO 360 ($4,997)

Full Funnel Map
1. Discovery
They find DJ through YouTube search ("IT to cybersecurity 2026"), an IG Reel in their feed, a LinkedIn post, a podcast episode during their commute, or a referral from a current student.
2. Trust
They watch 3-5 YouTube videos. They see DJ's case studies: Central Bank policy, the $300K wire fraud, Emirates Nuclear. They see student results: Rob G went from chef to $120K analyst. They think: "This guy actually did it."
3. Application
They click the link in the YouTube description or IG bio. They fill out a 5-8 question application: current role, years of experience, salary goal, timeline, whether they can invest $5K. GHL auto-scores them (0-12 pts).
4. Strategy Call
15 minutes on Zoom. Not a sales pitch. DJ reviews their application, gives an honest assessment. If they're a fit: "I'd like to invite you to CISO 360." If not: "Here's what to do first." The credibility does the selling.
5. Enrollment
$4,997 PIF or $1,897 x 3. Stripe checkout. GHL sends welcome email. DJ does a VIP welcome call with the new student.
6. Delivery
14-week CISO 360 program. 120+ pages of courseware (already built). Weekly live sessions. LinkedIn/resume optimization. Placement support. Result: student lands $150K+ cybersecurity role.
7. Flywheel
Student gets the result. DJ asks for a testimonial. Testimonial becomes a YouTube video. More proof, more applications, more students. Repeat.

Channel Strategy

YouTube (start week 1)

YouTube is a search engine. "IT to cybersecurity" peaked at 100 on Google Trends. Boyd Clewis has 318K subs in this niche. Josh Madakor has 220K. The demand is there.

  • 1 long video per week (10-20 min)
  • 3-5 Shorts per week (cut from the long video)
  • Every description links to the application page
  • Channel name: "DJ Naronikar | CISO Career Coach"
  • Thumbnails: DJ's face + bold text + one number

Instagram (start week 1)

Catches people scrolling. They see something interesting, click through to YouTube for the full video.

  • Repost YouTube Shorts as Reels. No extra work.
  • 1-2 carousels per week (career tips, salary data, myth-busting)
  • Bio links to YouTube channel
  • Every Reel ends with "Full breakdown on YouTube, link in bio"

LinkedIn (start week 1)

DJ already has 9K followers and 1K newsletter subscribers. This is where his audience lives.

  • 3-4 text posts per week (repurpose YouTube topics)
  • 1 newsletter per week through "Quantum AI Cyber Articles"
  • Every post points to the YouTube video in comments

Podcast (start week 2-3)

"Cyber Caffeine" is taken on Spotify (SISAP, 30 episodes). DJ needs a new name: "The CISO Path," "CISO 360 Podcast," or "From IT to CISO with DJ Naronikar."

  • Strip audio from the YouTube video. Upload as a podcast episode.
  • Same content, new channel, zero extra recording time.
  • Publish via Buzzsprout or Anchor (one upload goes everywhere)
  • Every intro/outro includes application CTA
