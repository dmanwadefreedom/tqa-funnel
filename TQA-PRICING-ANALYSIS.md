## HORMOZI ON SUBSCRIPTION VS ONE-TIME

Source: $100M Offers, Advanced Offer Stacking, LinkedIn pricing post

**Hormozi's actual recommendation:**
- **One-time payment** for upfront value delivery (courses, content, training)
- **Recurring payment** for ongoing value (support, community, access, updates)
- **HYBRID is optimal:** one-time core purchase + recurring continuity program

**His data on churn:**
- Monthly billing: 10.7% churn
- Quarterly: 5% churn
- Annual: 2% churn
- Monthly LTV: $950 average
- Annual LTV: $5,000 average

**His rule:** "Subscriptions work when there's a genuine ongoing reason to stay. If the core value is delivered in month 1, they'll cancel in month 2."

**The pattern:** The highest-revenue players (Boyd, SANS) use one-time payments. The lower-revenue players use monthly subscriptions. Monthly subs work for SCALE plays (Google, Cybrary) where they have millions of users. For high-touch coaching, one-time dominates.

## DJ'S CONCERN IS VALID

DJ said: "$97/mo with AI assistant + WhatsApp + weekly tasks — they'll feel they have everything and never upgrade to $499 or $4,999."

**This is a real cannibalization problem.** If the $97/mo includes:
- AI career assistant (DJ's brain)
- WhatsApp access to DJ
- Weekly task list
- Cert roadmap
- Monthly updates

...then what does the $499 course add? More videos? Raj already has an AI that answers all his questions. The $499 course becomes redundant.

**Dylan's counter is also valid:** "Most people don't take action. They'll pay $97/mo and not do anything, then eventually want more help."

This is true — online course completion rates are 5-15%. But that's a BAD business model to build on. You're betting that your product fails so people upgrade. That's not a Grand Slam Offer — that's a bait and switch.

## THE CORRECT ANSWER: OPTION C — THE HYBRID

Based on Boyd's model, Hormozi's framework, market data, AND DJ's feedback:

**Separate the CONTENT from the COMMUNITY.**

| Tier | Type | Price | What They Get |
|------|------|-------|--------------|
| AI-Proof Career Plan | ONE-TIME | $47 | Personalized PDF (scorecard, cert roadmap, salary, action items) |
| TQA Community | RECURRING | $27-$47/mo | WhatsApp group, AI assistant, monthly updates, accountability |
| Jumpstart Course | ONE-TIME | $499 | DJ's 5 videos + certificate + course material |
| Full 14-Week Program | ONE-TIME | $4,999 | Everything: mentorship, placement, guarantee |

### Why this works:

**$47 AI-Proof Career Plan (one-time):**
- Tripwire. Covers ad spend. Already built.
- Gives them the MAP but not the TRAINING.
- No cannibalization — it's a diagnostic, not a program.

**$27-$47/mo Community (recurring):**
- This is NOT the training. This is the GYM MEMBERSHIP.
- WhatsApp group with DJ's weekly voice notes
- AI assistant for quick questions
- Monthly market updates
- Peer accountability
- They can USE this alongside any tier — it's supplemental, not core
- At $27-$47/mo it's truly a no-brainer AND it doesn't compete with the $499 course
- DJ's concern solved: the community doesn't include the course content

**$499 Jumpstart Course (one-time):**
- THIS is where DJ's 5 videos live.
- This is the TRAINING that actually moves them toward a cybersecurity job.
- Pay once, get all 5 videos (or progressive unlock at $99/video — DJ's preference)
- Certificate at the end
- NOT gated behind the community — it's a separate purchase
- DJ: "This is the personal trainer at the gym"

**$4,999 Full Program (one-time):**
- The works. DJ's mentorship. Placement. Guarantee.
- For people who want done-WITH-you, not DIY.

### The math comparison:

**Option A (DJ's $99/video):**
- 20 buyers x 2 average videos completed = $198 avg x 20 = $3,960
- No recurring. No community. Dead after 5 weeks.

**Option B (Dylan's $97/mo):**
- 20 buyers x $97 x 3 months avg retention = $5,820
- Recurring but cannibalizes $499 course.

**Option C (Hybrid):**
- 30 buyers of $47 plan = $1,410 (covers ads)
- 15 join $37/mo community = $555/mo recurring = $6,660/year
- 10 buy $499 course = $4,990
- 3 upgrade to $4,999 = $14,997
- **Total first-year from same 30 people: $28,057**
- Plus the community keeps generating $555/mo FOREVER

### DJ's gym analogy made perfect by the hybrid:
- $47 = the fitness assessment (you know where you stand)
- $37/mo = the gym membership (access, environment, motivation)
- $499 = the personal trainer (structured program, expert guidance)
- $4,999 = the elite coaching package (everything, guaranteed results)

You can join the gym without a trainer. You can hire a trainer without the elite package. Each tier stands alone AND feeds the next. No cannibalization.

## WHY $27-$47/MO INSTEAD OF $97/MO

DJ said $97/mo is "not much." He's right from HIS perspective (he charges $2-3K/day). But from Raj's perspective:

- $97/mo = $1,164/year. That's a real subscription. His wife asks about it.
- $37/mo = $444/year. That's a Netflix + Spotify. Nobody questions it.
- Boyd's starter tier WAS $97/mo — he apparently DISCONTINUED it. Now it's just $2,997 or nothing. That tells you $97/mo didn't work for him either.
- Cyberkraft charges $27/mo for basic, $247/mo for elite. The $27 tier gets volume.
- Google charges $49/mo. With Google's brand.

At $37/mo, the community:
1. Gets MORE members (lower barrier)
2. Has lower churn (less painful to forget about)
3. Doesn't compete with the $499 course (different price point, different value)
4. Still generates meaningful recurring revenue at scale (100 members = $3,700/mo)
5. Gives DJ a permanent audience to sell $499 courses and $4,999 programs into
