**Primary belief built:** Belief 6 — A structured program is worth $5K
**Secondary beliefs reinforced:** 5 (DJ is the guide), 7 (spouse ROI math)

**Tone:** DJ speaking directly to camera. Conversational but authoritative. Not salesy. Not hype. A CISO explaining the business case for a career decision — the way he'd present to a board.

## SECTION 2: THE PROBLEM — STATED WITH DATA (1:30 - 4:00)

**Goal:** Make Raj feel SEEN. Validate the pain with numbers, not emotion.

---

"Here's what I see every week.

I talk to IT professionals who are good at what they do. 10, 15 years of experience. Solid technical skills. But they've hit a ceiling.

The salary ceiling is real. In IT infrastructure and support, the median salary flattens between $85,000 and $115,000. You can job-hop. You can get certifications. But the ceiling doesn't move much.

And now there's a second pressure: AI.

I'm not going to fear-monger about AI. But I will tell you what's happening in the companies I consult with. L1 support tickets are being automated. Network monitoring is being automated. Basic server administration is being handled by AI-assisted tools.

The roles that require human judgment — especially around RISK — those are growing. The roles that follow scripts and runbooks? Those are shrinking.

Here's the other side:

Right now, 750,000 cybersecurity positions in the US are unfilled. That's not a LinkedIn stat — that's NIST and CyberSeek data. Twenty-six percent of positions remain unfilled even though companies are actively trying to hire.

The average cybersecurity manager salary is $185,000. And that's the AVERAGE — not the ceiling.

So on one side, you have a field that's compressing. On the other, a field that can't hire fast enough. And the bridge between them? It's shorter than most people think.

That's what I want to show you."

## SECTION 3: WHY MOST PEOPLE DON'T MAKE IT (4:00 - 6:30)

**Goal:** Address the "I'll do it myself" objection BEFORE it forms.

---

"Now, you might be thinking — I'll just get my Security+ and start applying.

And I wish it were that simple. I really do. Because the information is out there. YouTube is free. Udemy courses are $12. CompTIA exams are $400.

But here's what happens to 90% of the people who take that path.

They start studying. They pass the certification — maybe in 6 months, maybe in a year. They update their LinkedIn. They start applying.

And they get silence. No interviews. No callbacks. Maybe a few automated rejections.

Why? Because the certification proves you studied. It doesn't prove you can do the job. And hiring managers — I know because I've been one for 28 years — hiring managers can tell the difference in 30 seconds.

They're looking for three things a certification can't give you:

First — can you translate technical risk into business language? CISOs don't talk about vulnerabilities. They talk about financial exposure, regulatory risk, board-level implications.

Second — do you understand how security fits into the business, not just the network? A firewall rule is technology. Knowing WHY that rule exists in the context of a $50M compliance obligation — that's cybersecurity.

Third — do you have a professional network that gets your resume past the ATS? Because 70% of security hires come through referrals, not job boards.

Self-study gives you none of these. And the longer you study alone, the more salary you're NOT earning in a role that pays $50,000 to $80,000 more per year.

I'll come back to that math in a minute."
