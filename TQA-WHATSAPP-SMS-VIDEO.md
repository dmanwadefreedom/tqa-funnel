### 1:00 — 3:00 | DJ'S STORY

"I started in cybersecurity before most people knew the word existed. Over 28 years, I've served as CISO and virtual CISO at Deloitte, EY, and Shell. I've worked across eight countries and assessed the security posture of over 400 organizations.

Last year, I wrote the artificial intelligence policy for a Middle East central bank. That policy is now the regulatory standard for every bank operating in that country. It's the kind of work companies pay me two to three thousand dollars a day to do.

So why am I running a Career Briefing for IT professionals?

Because I've spent three decades on the hiring side of cybersecurity. I've reviewed thousands of resumes. I've sat on hundreds of interview panels. And I've watched talented IT professionals — people with 8, 10, 15 years of experience — get stuck. Not because they lack the ability. Because nobody showed them how the cybersecurity career path actually works.

The hiring process in cybersecurity is different from general IT. The certifications that matter are different. The way you position your experience is different. And most of what people read online about breaking in is either outdated or written by someone who's never actually hired a security professional.

I built this briefing because I was tired of watching good people waste years on the wrong approach."

---

### 3:00 — 5:00 | WHAT THE BRIEFING COVERS

"Here is exactly what happens on April 18.

I'm going to walk you through the cybersecurity career path — not in theory, with real salary data at every level. Entry analyst roles. Mid-level engineering. Architecture. Leadership. CISO track. You'll see the actual numbers and know which path fits your background.

Then I'll cover the certification roadmap. I'll tell you which certifications actually influence hiring decisions and which ones are a waste of your money. I've reviewed over 5,000 resumes. I know what hiring managers look at and what they skip.

I'll explain how the hidden job market works. Most cybersecurity roles never make it to a job board. I'll show you where they live and how to access them.

And here's the part that makes this different from a YouTube video: we're going to do a live exercise. You'll need an open LinkedIn tab. I'm going to walk you through auditing your own profile in real time — the same audit I do when I'm evaluating candidates. You'll leave with specific changes to make, not general advice.

This is not motivational content. I'm not going to tell you to believe in yourself. You're a working professional — I respect your intelligence. You'll leave with a concrete next step. That's the standard."

I say it so you know that what you're going to hear on April 18 comes from someone who's still in the room — not someone who read about it."

"Here's what I need from you.

Show up live. Not the replay — live. The LinkedIn audit exercise only works in real time. I'll be looking at profiles and giving feedback on the spot. That doesn't translate to a recording.

### PRODUCTION NOTES

- **Record once.** This video is reusable for every future briefing. Only the date needs updating.
- **Delivery:** Send via email immediately after registration (GHL automation). Embed on the thank-you page. Send again 2 days before the briefing as a reminder.
- **Hosting:** Upload to YouTube as unlisted. Fast load, easy to embed, no hosting costs.
- **Wardrobe:** Professional but not corporate. Blazer or button-down. The viewer is an IT professional in a polo — meet them one step above, not three.
- **Background:** Clean. Bookshelf or office setting. Nothing distracting.
- **Delivery style:** Direct to camera. No teleprompter stiffness — DJ should read through the script 2-3 times, then deliver from memory using bullet points on a monitor behind the camera. The goal is conversational authority, not recitation.
- **No B-roll needed.** This is a trust video, not a brand video. DJ talking directly to the viewer is the entire point.
